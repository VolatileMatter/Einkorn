Einkorn Burger Buns
Greek yogurt dough — soft, protein-rich, no yeast required

These buns use a two-ingredient base that has quietly become one of the most useful things in the nourishing kitchen: einkorn flour and Greek yogurt. The yogurt brings the protein and acidity that would normally require either a long ferment or a carefully tended enriched dough. The result is a bun that is soft and pillowy with a slight tang, considerably more nutritious than anything from a bag, and ready in under an hour if you want it to be. Add sourdough discard if you have it — the fermentation further breaks down the grain and deepens the flavour. If you don't have it, skip it without apology.

The full burger as written here uses caramelized onions cooked slowly in bone broth and seasoned pastured beef — a meal that happens to be fast food by another name, made from ingredients that actually nourish the body. The bun-to-burger relationship is everything: these buns hold together under the weight of the whole enterprise without disintegrating, which is more than can be said for most commercial options.

For the Buns (makes 4)

Ingredient
Amount & Notes
Einkorn all-purpose flour
240g (2 cups) — plus more for dusting
Grass-fed Greek yogurt
300g (1¼ cups) — full fat, not strained dry
Sourdough starter or discard
60g (¼ cup) — optional; substitute equal weight extra yogurt
Baking powder
2 tsp (8g)
Fine sea salt
½ tsp
Egg wash
1 egg beaten with 1 tbsp milk — optional, for shine
✦ The Sourdough Option — Overnight Ferment

If you want the full benefit of fermentation — lower glycemic index, improved mineral absorption, easier digestion — add the sourdough discard and let the dough rest. Combine all ingredients, cover, and refrigerate overnight (8–12 hours) before shaping and baking. The flavour will deepen considerably and the texture becomes even more tender. Einkorn absorbs liquid slowly, so an overnight rest also allows the hydration to fully equalize.

If baking same-day without fermentation, the buns are still excellent — just slightly more bread-like and less tangy in character.

For the Burgers (makes 4 patties)

Ingredient
Amount & Notes
Pastured ground beef
450g (1 lb) — 80/20 fat ratio ideal
Fine sea salt
½ tsp
Black pepper
¼ tsp
Paprika
½ tsp — smoked or sweet
Garlic powder
½ tsp
Dried oregano or rosemary
1 tsp

Method

The Buns

  1. In a large bowl, combine the einkorn flour, baking powder, and salt. Whisk to distribute evenly.
  2. Add the Greek yogurt and sourdough discard (if using). Mix with a fork, then use your hands to bring the dough together into a ball. Einkorn absorbs liquid more slowly than standard flour — if the dough feels sticky after a minute of working, add flour one tablespoon at a time. If it feels too stiff, add yogurt one tablespoon at a time. The final dough should be soft, slightly tacky, and hold its shape without spreading.
  3. For overnight ferment: cover the dough and refrigerate. Proceed with shaping the following day.
  4. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Divide into 4 equal pieces. Flatten each piece gently with your palm into a disc about ¾ inch thick and 4 inches across. The discs should be wider than you think — they puff upward during baking and will reach the right height and diameter naturally.
  6. Place on the prepared baking sheet. Brush the tops generously with egg wash if using — this gives the buns their golden sheen.
  7. Bake for 22–25 minutes, until the tops are golden and a toothpick inserted in the centre comes out clean. Transfer to a wire rack and cool at least 10 minutes before slicing — the interior needs time to set.

The Caramelized Onions (start these before the buns)

  1. Heat oil or butter in a wide skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions and a pinch of salt. Spread evenly and let sit undisturbed for 2–3 minutes before stirring.
  2. Add half the bone broth. Sauté, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes — the onions should begin to soften and take on some colour.
  3. Add the remaining broth to deglaze the pan (scrape up any darkened bits — that's flavour). Continue cooking for another 10 minutes, reducing heat slightly if the pan looks dry, until the onions are deep golden, silky, and sweet. Set aside.

The Burgers

  1. Combine the ground beef with all seasonings. Mix gently with your hands — just enough to distribute the seasoning without compressing the meat too much, which makes for a denser patty.
  2. Divide into 4 equal portions and form into patties slightly wider than the buns (they will shrink as they cook). Press a shallow thumbprint in the centre of each patty — this prevents them from puffing up into a dome.
  3. Grill or cook in a cast iron skillet over medium-high heat to your preferred doneness. Do not press the patties with a spatula — this forces out the juices. Season with a little extra salt as they come off the heat.

Assembly

Slice the cooled buns in half. Place the burger on the bottom bun, heap with caramelized onions, add any other toppings you love (lettuce, pickles, a good quality ketchup without added sugar), and close. These buns hold well under moisture — they will not dissolve under the weight of a properly juicy burger.

✦ Notes on the Greek Yogurt Dough
  • Fat content matters. Full-fat Greek yogurt gives the best texture. Low-fat versions will produce a slightly drier, denser bun. If your yogurt is very thick (strained), stir in a tablespoon of milk to loosen it before mixing.
  • Einkorn absorbs slowly. Do not judge the dough's hydration until you have worked it for at least 2 minutes. What feels sticky at first will become much more manageable as the flour absorbs the yogurt. Resist adding too much extra flour early.
  • Oat flour option: Oat flour can be substituted 1:1 for a different texture — slightly more tender and with a milder flavour. The dough will be softer and may need a few extra minutes in the oven.
  • Size variation: For slider buns, divide into 8 pieces. Reduce bake time to 18–20 minutes.
