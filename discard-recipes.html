Discard Recipes
The art of wasting nothing — crackers, cornbread, pancakes, and more

On the prairie, no food would ever go to waste. The sourdough discard that accumulates with each feeding is alive, nutritious, and versatile. It lacks the strength to leaven a loaf on its own, but it gives crackers their snap, cornbread its tang, and pancakes their depth.

✦ When to Use Discard

Days 1–3 of a new starter: the discard is essentially just flour and water with minimal fermentation. You can still use it, but you won't get the flavour benefits or the fermentation-related digestive benefits yet.

Established discard: kept in the fridge, collected over days, is your best version. It is tangy, slightly fermented, and adds genuine flavour and the mildest probiotic benefit to anything you add it to.

✦

I. Sourdough Discard Crackers

These require no rise and come together in under an hour. The ratio is simple and scales freely: 4 parts discard to 1 part fat by weight.

Ingredients

Ingredient
Amount
Einkorn sourdough discard
200g (small batch) or 500g (double batch)
Olive oil or melted butter
50g / 125g — always at 4:1 ratio by weight
Fine sea salt
Pinch in the batter + flaky salt on top
Seasonings
Everything bagel, rosemary, za'atar, sesame, chilli, or plain

Method

  1. Whisk together discard and fat until smooth. Stir in any herbs that won't burn on top.
  2. Line a baking sheet with a silicone mat or parchment. Pour batter in rough parallel lines with gaps between them for airflow.
  3. Smear flat and thin with a spatula. Sprinkle finishing salt and heat-stable seasonings on top.
  4. Bake at 350°F for 25–35 minutes. Swap tray positions halfway if using two trays.
  5. The snap test: try to break the cracker at its thickest point. If it snaps cleanly, it is done. If it bends, return to the oven 5 more minutes.
  6. Cool fully on a rack — they crisp further as they cool. Store airtight up to 5 days.
✦

II. Einkorn Sourdough Cornbread

A warm, golden comfort bread that comes together quickly with discard — no rise required. Sweetened with honey only.

Ingredients (8×8 pan or 10-inch cast iron)

Ingredient
Amount
Yellow cornmeal
1 cup
Whole grain einkorn flour
1 cup
Baking powder
1 tbsp
Fine sea salt
½ tsp
Grass-fed butter (softened)
3 tbsp
Local honey
⅓ cup
Eggs (room temperature)
2 large
Sourdough discard
1 cup — active or inactive
Cheddar cheese (optional)
1 cup shredded — highly recommended

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Grease your pan or skillet.
  2. Whisk together dry ingredients (cornmeal, flour, baking powder, salt).
  3. Cream butter and honey. Add eggs one at a time.
  4. Alternately fold in the sourdough discard and dry ingredients in two or three additions. Mix gently until just combined — do not over-mix.
  5. Fold in cheese if using.
  6. Pour into the greased pan. Bake 20–25 minutes until golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
✦

III. Sourdough Discard Pancakes

The classic discard recipe. Fermented discard gives pancakes a depth of flavour that commercial batter cannot replicate. Best made with mature discard that has been in the fridge a day or two.

Ingredients (serves 4–6)

Ingredient
Amount
Sourdough discard
2 cups (about 460g)
Eggs
2 large
Coconut oil or melted butter
¼ cup (4 tbsp)
Honey
2 tbsp
Fine sea salt
½ tsp
Baking soda
1 tsp
  1. Whisk together discard, eggs, fat, honey, and salt until smooth.
  2. Add baking soda last — the mixture will bubble as the soda reacts with the acid in the discard. This is your lift.
  3. Cook immediately on a preheated cast iron pan over medium heat. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, then flip once.
✦

IV. The Fried Discard Snack

This is a principle rather than a recipe. Pour active discard directly into a hot pan with olive oil or butter. Scatter chopped chives or scallions, a pinch of za'atar or any spice you like, and fry until the edges are crispy and the centre is set. It is fermented fried bread — one of the most satisfying five-minute meals in this entire book.

✦

V. Sourdough Discard Pizza Crust

Preheat a pizza stone or cast iron skillet to 450°F — it must be very hot. Add sourdough starter (discard or active) directly to the hot surface, shape it into a rough circle, drizzle with olive oil, salt, and herbs, and bake 8–12 minutes. The high heat crisps the base before the top can dry out. Add your toppings after the first bake, then return to the oven until the cheese is bubbling. This works beautifully with discard because you don't need rise — the high heat does everything.
