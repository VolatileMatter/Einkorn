Einkorn Hand Pies & Empanadas
Meat-stuffed pastries in two traditions — medieval and Iberian

The hand pie is one of the oldest prepared foods — portable, filling, warming, and endlessly adaptable. From the medieval meat pie to the Cornish pasty to the Latin American empanada, every culture that has combined pastry and filling has arrived at essentially the same perfect thing. Sourdough discard makes excellent pastry: the mild acidity gives a subtle tang, and the moisture means it comes together without cracking.

The Pastry (makes 8 large pies or 12 empanadas)

Ingredient
Amount & Notes
Einkorn all-purpose flour
300g — plus more for dusting
Whole grain einkorn flour
100g
Sourdough discard
150g — cold from the fridge is fine
Cold butter or lard
120g — cubed, very cold; lard for flakiness, butter for flavour
Cold water
3–5 tbsp — add only as needed
Fine sea salt
1 tsp
Egg wash
1 egg beaten with a splash of water

Making the Pastry

  1. Combine both flours and salt. Add cold fat cubes. Rub with fingertips until the mixture resembles rough breadcrumbs — work quickly to keep fat cold.
  2. Add the sourdough discard. Mix gently with a fork. Add cold water one tablespoon at a time until the dough just comes together. Do not overwork.
  3. Press into a flat disc, wrap, and refrigerate at least 30 minutes (or up to 24 hours). Cold pastry rolls more easily and gives a flakier result.

Filling Options

FillingIngredientsNotes
Medieval Spiced Pork200g ground pork, pinch each ginger / cinnamon / black pepper, small handful currants, saltHistorically accurate — medieval cooks used spices freely in savoury pies; currants against spiced meat is extraordinary
Spiced Beef or Lamb250g ground beef or lamb, 1 small onion, 1 tsp cumin, ½ tsp coriander, saltCook through fully before filling; cool completely before assembling
Argentine Empanada200g ground beef, ½ onion, 2 garlic cloves, 1 tsp paprika (sweet & smoked), 1 hard-boiled egg chopped, handful green olives, saltThe egg and olives are traditional; a classic combination
Herb Chicken200g shredded roast chicken, 2 tbsp soft cheese, fresh thyme & parsley, saltUse leftover roast chicken — no extra cooking needed
Cheese & Onion2 large onions slowly caramelised (45 min), 150g strong cheddar or gruyèreMeatless; sweet, rich, deeply satisfying

Assembling & Baking

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll pastry to about ⅛ inch thick. Cut into rounds: 5–6 inch for hand pies, 4-inch for empanadas.
  3. Place a generous spoonful of cooled filling on one half of each round, leaving a 1cm border. Do not overfill — this causes bursting.
  4. Brush the border with egg wash. Fold pastry over to form a half-moon. Press edges firmly. Crimp with a fork, or fold and pinch a rope braid for the traditional empanada edge.
  5. Place on lined tray. Brush tops with egg wash. Cut two small slits in each for steam.
  6. Bake 25–30 minutes until deeply golden. Cool 10 minutes before eating.
✦ Medieval Spice Notes

Medieval cooks used ginger, cinnamon, and pepper in savoury meat pies far more generously than we do today. These spices were expensive — their presence was a mark of quality and care. A small pinch of each in your meat filling is worth trying. The sweetness of currants against the spiced meat is a combination that has been lost to time and deserves to be recovered.
