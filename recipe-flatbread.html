Rustic Pilgrim Flatbread
The oldest bread in the world — two ingredients, one hot stone

Evidence of flatbread dates back 14,000 years. Viking women cooked it on hot stones. Pioneer women baked it on coals. This is the bread every civilisation has made, stripped to its essence. No starter, no rise, no equipment beyond a hot pan.

✦ The Viking Way

For the most authentic flavour, add a splash of real ale or beer to the water — unfiltered ale adds a subtle depth immediately reminiscent of something very old. Freshly milled whole grain einkorn gives this bread a rustic, satisfying texture that pre-sifted commercial flour cannot replicate. Serve with soft cheese, smoked fish, or honey and butter — as it would have been eaten by the fire in a Norse longhouse.

Ingredients — Plain Flatbread (makes 6–8)

Ingredient
Amount & Notes
Whole grain einkorn flour
240g (2 cups) — freshly milled gives best flavour
Filtered water
120g (½ cup) warm
Fine sea salt
1 tsp
Butter or lard (optional)
1 tbsp — fry in this for richer flavour
Ale or beer (optional)
Replace a splash of water for the Viking tradition

Method — Plain

  1. Combine flour and salt. Add water gradually, mixing until a rough dough forms. All flour must be hydrated.
  2. Rest 30 minutes. The dough will become softer and more pliable — do not skip this.
  3. Heat a dry cast iron pan to medium-high. No oil needed for plain flatbread; a small amount of butter or lard gives a richer result.
  4. Pinch off a golf-ball-sized piece of dough. On a lightly floured surface, flatten with your palm and roll thin — aim for about ⅛ inch thick.
  5. Lay in the hot pan. After 15–30 seconds the underside will be set. Flip. Repeat.
  6. As bubbles form, press gently with a spatula. Keep flipping every 30 seconds until both sides show golden-brown patches and the bread feels dry to the touch.

Sourdough Discard Version

This version uses sourdough discard in place of some of the water, giving the flatbreads a deeper, more complex flavour and the added nutritional benefits of fermentation. The method is nearly identical — the only real difference is a longer rest, which allows the acid in the discard to work on the flour.

Ingredient
Amount & Notes
Whole grain einkorn flour
220g — slightly less than the plain version
Sourdough discard
100g — straight from the fridge is fine
Filtered water
60–80g warm — add slowly, the discard brings moisture
Fine sea salt
1 tsp
Butter or lard (optional)
1 tbsp — for frying
  1. Combine flour and salt. Add the discard and mix until shaggy. Add water gradually — the dough should be soft and pliable, not sticky. Einkorn absorbs liquid slowly; give it a minute before adding more.
  2. Cover and rest 1 hour at room temperature, or overnight in the refrigerator. The longer rest noticeably deepens the flavour. The dough will relax and become easier to roll.
  3. Cook exactly as the plain version: roll thin, hot dry pan, flip every 30 seconds until golden patches appear.
✦ Make-Ahead Option

The discard dough can be made the night before and refrigerated overnight. Pull it out 20 minutes before cooking to take the chill off. The extra rest produces flatbreads with noticeably more flavour and a slightly more tender texture.

Eat warm. Best on the day they are made, though they toast beautifully the next morning if any survive.
