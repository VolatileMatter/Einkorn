Einkorn Chicken & Dumplings
A Great Depression staple made nourishing — deep broth, pillowy dumplings

Chicken and dumplings is one of the great American comfort foods — a Depression-era dish built from almost nothing that manages to taste like everything. This version uses einkorn flour for the dumplings, which gives them a tender, nutty depth that standard flour cannot match. The dumplings float on top of the broth like soft biscuits born of steam, golden and yielding, with a glossy exterior and a dry, cloud-like interior. Make the broth from scratch if you have the time. You will taste the difference in every spoonful.

Sourdough Discard Dumplings

If you have sourdough discard on hand, use it here. The dumplings become noticeably more tender and flavourful — the acidity in the discard reacts with the baking powder to give a more vigorous rise. The discard replaces some of the flour and milk, so adjust accordingly.

Ingredient

Butter (melted)
3 tbsp

¼–⅓ cup — add slowly, the discard brings moisture
Fresh parsley (optional)
¼ cup chopped

Mix exactly as the plain version: combine dry ingredients, add discard and butter, add milk sparingly until the batter is wet and sticky but not liquid. Drop into simmering soup and cook covered for 15 minutes without lifting the lid.

Method

Step 1 — Make the Broth (1 hour, mostly hands-off)

  1. Place the whole chicken in a large Dutch oven or soup pot. Add celery, carrots, onion, salt, and peppercorns. Pour in 8–10 cups of water — the chicken doesn't need to be fully submerged.
  2. Bring to a lively simmer, then reduce heat to maintain a gentle, steady simmer. Cover and cook for approximately 1 hour, until the chicken is cooked through and the meat is pulling away from the bone easily.
  3. Remove the chicken carefully and set aside to cool. Strain the broth through a colander into a large bowl, discarding the spent vegetables. You should have roughly 8 cups of golden, fragrant broth. Reserve the bones for a second batch of stock if you wish.
  4. Once the chicken is cool enough to handle, shred all the meat from the bones using two forks. Keep pieces on the larger side — they'll continue to break down in the soup.

Step 2 — Build the Soup (30 minutes)

  1. Melt butter in the same pot over medium heat. Add carrots, celery, onion, and a pinch of salt. Sauté 6–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are beginning to soften and the onion is translucent.
  2. Add minced garlic, oregano, and thyme. Stir and cook one further minute until fragrant.
  3. Add salt, pepper, and the 4 tablespoons of einkorn flour. Stir until the flour coats the vegetables evenly. This step ensures the flour loses its raw taste before the broth goes in.
  4. Pour in all 8 cups of broth and add the shredded chicken. Stir well. Add spinach if using. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook for 20–25 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning.

Step 3 — Make the Dumplings

  1. While the soup simmers, make the dumpling batter. Whisk together the einkorn flour, baking powder, salt, and parsley (and Parmigiano if using) in a medium bowl.
  2. Add the melted butter and cold milk. Stir just until combined — the dough should be wet, sticky, and a little rough-looking. Do not overwork it. If it seems too stiff, add milk one tablespoon at a time. Einkorn absorbs liquid more slowly than standard flour, so let the dough rest 2 minutes before judging.
  3. When the soup has simmered its full time, make sure it is at a gentle simmer (not a rolling boil — too vigorous will cause the dumplings to crack). Use a large ice cream scoop or a generous spoon to drop portions of dough directly onto the surface of the soup. They should float. If one sinks, nudge it gently back to the surface.
  4. Do not stir the soup after the dumplings are in. Place the lid on and simmer undisturbed for 15 minutes, until the dumplings are cooked through. They will have a glossy, wet appearance on the outside and a dry, biscuit-like texture inside when broken open.
  5. Serve immediately: scoop one or two dumplings into each bowl and ladle soup around them. Scatter fresh parsley over the top.
✦ Notes on Einkorn Dumplings
  • Weigh the flour. Einkorn dumplings are sensitive to hydration. Too much flour and they will be dense and chewy. Use a scale: 250g is the correct amount for 6 servings.
  • Do not stir. Once the dumplings are in, resist the temptation to stir. They need to steam in place to set properly.
  • The broth must be simmering, not boiling. A rolling boil will cause the dumplings to break apart. Gentle and steady is the key.
  • Time-saving option: Use a store-bought rotisserie chicken and good-quality bone broth. The flavour is less complex but still excellent — ideal for a weeknight.
  • Storage: This soup keeps beautifully refrigerated for 2–3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop. The dumplings will soften slightly but remain delicious.
