The Everyday Einkorn Sourdough Loaf
A rustic boule with deep flavour — the bread that anchors your week

This follows a two-day rhythm: mix and fold on Day 1, bake on Day 2. The overnight cold proof improves both flavour and oven spring, and fits naturally into the flow of a household. All phytic acid breakdown occurs during this extended fermentation — this is nutritional alchemy as much as baking.

Schedule Overview

Day 1
Morning
Feed your starter — it needs 4–8 hours to reach peak activity.
4–8 hrs later

Add starter & salt — mix gently until incorporated.
Next 2 hrs
Stretch & fold — 3–4 rounds, 20–30 min apart.
4–8 hrs

Evening

Day 2 Morning
Bake — straight from the fridge into the hot Dutch oven.

Ingredients

Ingredient
Amount & Notes
Einkorn all-purpose flour
420g — Jovial or freshly milled
Whole grain einkorn flour
180g — fresh-milled preferred
Filtered water
400g, warm (~85°F)
Active sourdough starter
120g — fed 4–8 hrs prior; doubled and bubbly
Fine sea salt
12g (about 2 tsp)

Method

Autolyse

  1. Combine both flours in a large glass bowl. Add the warm water and mix until no dry patches remain. Dough will be sticky and rough.
  2. Cover and rest 45–60 minutes.

Add Starter & Salt

  1. Add the active starter and salt over the autolysed dough.
  2. Fold and mix gently until incorporated — about 2 minutes. Do not over-mix. The dough will look rough. That is correct.
  3. Cover tightly. Rest 30 minutes.
⚠ Do Not Over-Mix

Once all ingredients are incorporated, stop. Einkorn dough worked too hard becomes gummy and will not rise properly. It should look rough — not smooth — at this stage. Rest and fermentation will do the work.

Stretch & Fold

  1. Wet your hands. Grab one edge, stretch firmly upward until resistance, fold over centre. Rotate 90°. Repeat all four sides. Flip into a rough ball. Cover. Rest 20–30 min.
  2. Repeat 2–3 more times. After the final round the dough should feel cohesive, show surface bubbles, and hold a slight dome.

Bulk Fermentation

  1. Cover tightly. Place in a warm spot (75–80°F). Ferment undisturbed until ~75% growth, bubbly, and the surface shows activity — 4–8 hours.
  2. If it is late and the dough is not ready: refrigerate overnight. Cold fermentation continues slowly. Resume in the morning at room temperature.

Shape & Cold Proof

  1. Turn the dough gently onto a lightly floured surface. Fold edges toward centre. Flip seam-down.
  2. Drag in a gentle circular motion with cupped hands to build surface tension until the surface is taut and smooth.
  3. Heavily flour a proofing basket or flour-lined bowl. Place dough seam-side UP. Cover. Refrigerate 8–14 hours.

Bake

  1. Place Dutch oven (lid on) in oven. Preheat to 500°F for at least 45–60 minutes.
  2. Invert cold dough onto parchment. Score at 45° with a sharp blade: one confident slash, ¼ inch deep through the centre.
  3. Lower into the hot Dutch oven on the parchment. Lid on. Bake 30 minutes.
  4. Remove lid. Reduce to 450°F. Bake 15–20 more minutes until deeply golden brown. Judge by colour.
  5. Optional curing step: oven off, door cracked, bread returned to oven for 20 minutes. Produces an extraordinary crackling crust.
  6. Cool on a wire rack a full hour before slicing.
