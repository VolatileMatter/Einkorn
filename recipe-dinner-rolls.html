Honey Einkorn Dinner Rolls
Soft, golden, sweet — same-day or make-ahead from frozen

Honey makes these rolls extraordinarily soft and helps the crust develop a beautiful golden colour. Combined with grass-fed butter or lard, the result has the quality of melting in the mouth — the kind of bread that makes a table feel like a feast.

Ingredients (makes 12 rolls)

Ingredient
Amount & Notes
Einkorn all-purpose flour
420g
Whole grain einkorn flour
80g
Filtered water (warm)
250g (~85°F)
Active sourdough starter
120g — fed and at peak
Warm whole milk
80g (⅓ cup)
Local honey
30g (1½ tbsp)
Butter (softened) or lard
40g (3 tbsp) — lard gives lighter crumb; butter more flavour
Fine sea salt
10g (1¾ tsp)
Egg wash
1 egg + 1 tbsp milk

Same-Day Method

  1. Combine both flours. Whisk warm water, milk, honey, and active starter. Pour wet into dry. Mix to a rough dough. Cover and rest 30 minutes (autolyse).
  2. Add softened fat and salt. Mix gently until incorporated.
  3. Perform 3 rounds of stretch-and-fold, 20–30 minutes apart.
  4. Cover and ferment in a warm spot until 50–75% grown and puffy — 3–5 hours.
  5. Turn out gently. Divide into 12 pieces (~80g each). Shape each: flatten slightly, fold edges toward centre, flip seam-down, roll with a cupped hand against the counter until smooth and taut.
  6. Arrange in a well-buttered 9×13 dish. Cover and proof at room temperature 60–90 minutes until noticeably puffed.
  7. Preheat oven to 375°F. Brush gently with egg wash. Bake 22–26 minutes until golden brown. Brush immediately with melted butter on removal.

Overnight Method (Better Flavour)

This is the preferred approach if you have the time. An overnight cold proof deepens the flavour considerably and makes the dough easier to shape — cold, well-developed dough holds its shape while it warms and rises in the baking dish. The enriched dough with honey and milk is particularly well suited to slow fermentation.

  1. Follow steps 1–5 of the same-day method above, through shaping.
  2. Arrange shaped rolls in a buttered 9×13 dish. Cover tightly with cling film or a lid and refrigerate overnight (8–16 hours).
  3. In the morning, remove from the refrigerator and let stand at room temperature for 45–60 minutes. The rolls will begin to puff slightly.
  4. Preheat oven to 375°F. Brush with egg wash. Bake 22–26 minutes until deep golden. Brush with melted butter immediately.
✦ Using Discard Instead of Active Starter

You can make a lighter, quicker version of these rolls using sourdough discard in place of active starter — but only if you also add 1 tsp of baking powder to the flour mix, since the discard does not have the leavening strength to lift an enriched dough on its own. The baking powder takes over the rise; the discard contributes flavour and nutrition. Use the same-day method and reduce the bulk fermentation to 1–2 hours. The flavour will be milder, the texture slightly less complex, but still considerably better than rolls made with commercial yeast.

Freeze-Ahead Options

Option A — Freeze After Baking

Bake fully. Cool completely. Freeze in a single layer, then bag. To serve: place frozen rolls on a tray and warm at 325°F for 12–15 minutes. Brush with butter as they come out.

Option B — Freeze Shaped, Unbaked (Best Results)
  1. Shape the rolls and arrange on a parchment-lined tray, not touching.
  2. Cover and refrigerate overnight (8–12 hours) — a cold proof instead of room-temp proofing.
  3. Freeze the tray 2 hours until solid. Transfer to a zip bag. Freeze up to 2 months.
  4. To bake: arrange in a buttered dish. Thaw overnight in the fridge or 3–4 hours on the counter. Bake as normal.
